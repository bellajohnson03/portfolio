Erie County Disaster Resilience Assessment

GIS-Based Tornado Vulnerability and Emergency Management Analysis

Project Overview

This project evaluated disaster resilience in Erie County, New York through a multi-hazard GIS assessment focused on hazard vulnerability, critical infrastructure, emergency services, and community resilience.

As part of a collaborative disaster resilience project, my individual focus examined tornado vulnerability and the role of GIS in supporting emergency management decision-making. The assessment incorporated spatial analysis of healthcare accessibility, infrastructure dependencies, population characteristics, and mitigation opportunities to identify factors that influence community preparedness and recovery.

Project Context

This collaborative project assessed multiple hazards affecting Erie County, including tornadoes, extreme weather, flooding, and lake-effect snow. Each team member evaluated a specific hazard area before integrating findings into a broader disaster resilience assessment. My primary focus was tornado risk, examining how hazard exposure, critical infrastructure, healthcare access, and community resources influence emergency response and long-term resilience.

The project utilized publicly available datasets and GIS tools to analyze vulnerability patterns and develop geospatial products that support emergency planning, mitigation strategies, and resource allocation.

Tools & Data

  • ArcGIS Pro
  • PolicyMap
  • FEMA Resilience Analysis and Planning Tool (RAPT)
  • U.S. Census Data
  • NOAA Tornado Data
  • Healthcare and critical infrastructure datasets

Analysis

GIS analysis was used to evaluate relationships between tornado vulnerability, hospital capacity, electric infrastructure, population characteristics, and community resources. The project examined how spatial patterns can influence emergency response, recovery planning, and long-term mitigation strategies.

Methodology

The project workflow involved collecting, preparing, and analyzing hazard, infrastructure, and demographic datasets to evaluate disaster resilience across Erie County, New York. Data sources included FEMA resilience indicators, NOAA hazard information, U.S. Census demographic data, healthcare datasets, and critical infrastructure layers.

Using GIS tools, spatial relationships were analyzed to evaluate emergency response capacity, community vulnerability, and mitigation opportunities. Analysis techniques included data integration, spatial visualization, buffer analysis, and comparison of infrastructure availability with population characteristics.

The final geospatial products supported three major emergency management phases: response, recovery, and mitigation. These analyses demonstrated how GIS can assist decision-makers in identifying vulnerable areas, prioritizing resources, and improving community resilience before and after disaster events.

Geospatial Products

The following maps were developed to support the disaster resilience assessment by analyzing healthcare accessibility, emergency response capabilities, population characteristics, and critical infrastructure throughout Erie County.

This map evaluates the distribution of hospitals and available bed capacity throughout Erie County to support emergency medical planning following tornado events. The analysis helps identify healthcare resources that may be critical during disaster response and patient surge operations.

This map examines the spatial distribution of fire stations to assess emergency response coverage across Erie County. Understanding station locations helps identify areas where response times and emergency service accessibility may be affected during severe weather events.

Population density was analyzed to identify areas where larger concentrations of residents may require additional emergency resources during tornado events. This information supports evacuation planning, resource allocation, and community preparedness efforts.

This map identifies critical infrastructure that supports essential community functions before, during, and after disasters. Mapping these facilities helps emergency managers evaluate vulnerabilities, prioritize protection efforts, and support long-term community resilience.

Key Findings

  • Healthcare accessibility and hospital capacity are important factors when planning emergency response operations following tornado events.
  • Critical infrastructure dependencies, including electric generation facilities, can influence continuity of emergency services.
  • Population characteristics and community resources contribute to differences in disaster resilience across Erie County.
  • GIS provides emergency managers with tools to visualize vulnerability patterns and prioritize mitigation strategies.
