Erie County Disaster Resilience Assessment

GIS-Based Tornado Vulnerability and Emergency Management Analysis

Project Overview

This project evaluated tornado risk and community resilience in Erie County, New York through a GIS-based assessment of hazard exposure, critical infrastructure, healthcare accessibility, and social vulnerability.

Project Context

As part of a multi-hazard disaster resilience assessment, my group evaluated different hazards affecting Erie County, including tornadoes, extreme weather, flooding, and lake effect snow. My individual focus was tornado risk assessment and the role of GIS in supporting emergency management decision-making.

Tools & Data

  • ArcGIS Pro
  • PolicyMap
  • FEMA Resilience Analysis and Planning Tool (RAPT)
  • U.S. Census Data
  • NOAA Tornado Data
  • Healthcare and critical infrastructure datasets

Analysis

GIS analysis was used to evaluate relationships between tornado vulnerability, hospital capacity, electric infrastructure, population characteristics, and community resources. The project examined how spatial patterns can influence emergency response, recovery planning, and long-term mitigation strategies.

Methodology

The project workflow involved collecting hazard, infrastructure, and demographic datasets and integrating them within ArcGIS Pro and PolicyMap to evaluate tornado vulnerability across Erie County, New York.

Spatial analysis focused on identifying relationships between tornado risk, healthcare accessibility, critical infrastructure, and community resilience. Maps were developed to support emergency management planning by highlighting areas where populations may experience increased challenges during response and recovery operations.

The assessment examined three major disaster management phases: response, recovery, and mitigation. Each phase incorporated GIS analysis to evaluate resource availability, infrastructure dependencies, and opportunities for improving community resilience.

Geospatial Products

Key Findings

  • Healthcare accessibility and hospital capacity are important factors when planning emergency response operations following tornado events.
  • Critical infrastructure dependencies, including electric generation facilities, can influence continuity of emergency services.
  • Population characteristics and community resources contribute to differences in disaster resilience across Erie County.
  • GIS provides emergency managers with tools to visualize vulnerability patterns and prioritize mitigation strategies.
