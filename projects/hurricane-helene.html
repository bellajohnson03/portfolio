Hurricane Helene Impact Assessment

Geospatial Analysis of Hazard, Infrastructure, and Community Resilience

Project Overview

This project uses GIS and ArcGIS StoryMaps to analyze the impacts of Hurricane Helene across Florida and Georgia. Multiple open-source datasets were integrated to evaluate storm hazards, emergency infrastructure, community vulnerability, and resilience factors.

Tools & Data

  • ArcGIS Pro
  • ArcGIS StoryMaps
  • ArcGIS Living Atlas
  • NOAA Hurricane Data
  • FEMA Resilience Data
  • U.S. Census ACS Data
  • U.S. Army Corps of Engineers Levee Data

Analysis

The project integrated hazard, infrastructure, and demographic datasets to evaluate Hurricane Helene impacts and communicate findings through interactive geospatial storytelling.

Geospatial Products

Hurricane Path & Affected Areas

Visualized Hurricane Helene’s storm track, wind impact zones, and affected regions using NOAA hurricane data.

Storm Surge & Flood Vulnerability

Integrated storm surge forecasts and flood-related datasets to evaluate areas exposed to hurricane impacts.

Emergency Healthcare Accessibility

Developed an interactive comparison of hospital locations and buffer zones to assess healthcare accessibility across impacted regions.

Community Resilience Analysis

Integrated FEMA Community Resilience Challenges Index data and demographic indicators to evaluate community vulnerability.

Skills Demonstrated

  • Hazard and vulnerability assessment
  • Critical infrastructure analysis
  • Spatial data visualization and cartographic design
  • Interactive web mapping with ArcGIS StoryMaps
  • Emergency management-focused GIS analysis

Methodology

The project workflow involved acquiring publicly available geospatial datasets, preparing and managing data in ArcGIS Pro, performing spatial analysis, and developing cartographic products for publication through ArcGIS StoryMaps.

Analysis included hurricane track visualization, storm surge assessment, hospital accessibility analysis using buffer zones, evacuation route mapping, community resilience evaluation, and infrastructure vulnerability assessment.
